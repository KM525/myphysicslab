The Euler-Lagrange Equation

The Euler-Lagrange equation is a differential equation that \(y(x)\) must satisfy in order to minimize (or possibly maximize) an integral of the form $$ I = \int_{x_1}^{x_2} f(x, y, y') dx $$ where also \(y(x)\) must pass through the two fixed end points \(y(x_1) = y_1\) and \(y(x_2) = y_2\). Through reasoning about families of curves that include the minimizing curve \(y(x)\), we can get the Euler-Lagrange equation: $$ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} - \frac{d}{dx} \left( \frac{\partial f}{\partial y'} \right) = 0 $$ #PAGE_INFO #LOREM_IPSUM #LOAD_APP #BUILD_APP #SHOW_ALL #START_APP #PAGE_FOOTER
